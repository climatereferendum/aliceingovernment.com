Frequently Asked Questions

Climate change is too important for us to rely only on governments to fix it. It is us, the citizens, that should decide how to respond to it. The goal of this platform is for you, me, and people from all over the world, to vote on how we think we should we react to Climate Change.

Your vote, like everyone else’s, will create a citizen consensus that can bring about all sorts of changes such as financing top solutions, adjusting public budgets, creating new projects, pressuring governments, adjusting international agreements, and whatever our collective imagination comes up with.

Phase 2 of this project will be all about choosing and putting those potential applications on track but for now, go explore our voters section to read about how other people think we should react to climate change, invite people that would enjoy sharing their opinion, and follow our updates on Twitter.

Despite there being more than one scientific study about how to respond to climate change effectively (visit Planetary Boundaries for an example), we chose Drawdown due to its 200+ team of researchers and academics, their robust legitimacy among the scientific community, and its inclusion of economic factors and feasibility studies. Other voters, users, and non-users of our platform might have a public interest in this voting process being permanent and immutable and thus removing voters might undermine the public interest and trust in this process.

For this you will need to fill a form and provide us with different kinds of identity papers for us to make sure you are who you say you are and to express your legitimate reasons to ask for the erasure of your data. Please send us an email to hello@aliceingovernment.com and we will send you the corresponding form. Without legitimate reasons, identity papers, or the erasure form filled, we will not be able to help you.

    The right to restrict processing - You have the right to request that Alice in Government restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

    The right to object to processing - You have the right to object to Alice in Government's processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

    The right to data portability - You have the right to request that Alice in Government transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, under certain conditions.

    If you make a request, we have one month to respond to you. If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us at our email: hello@aliceingovernment.com
